My name is Hamed Saleh, and I am a PhD student in the CS Department at the University of Maryland. I am working under the supervision of Prof. Hajiaghayi.

links:

I am interested in combinatorial algorithms in sublinear models, such as MPC, Streaming, and Sublinear-space. We often study graph algorithms, and in some cases simple DP problems, in those sublinear models. I also think abstract game theoretic problems are cute. I sometimes participate or get involved in competitive programming competitions.

My dissertation is titled "Sublinear Algorithms for Processing Massive Datasets", and the proposal and the slides are available.

Task Preparation System, tps.
with Kian Mirjalali
tps is a command-line interface for developing IOI/ICPC-like problems, with a workflow mainly inspired by Polygon. It has been used to prepare the tasks in IOIs (2017, 2019, 2020), Iranian National Olympiad in Informatics, and ICPC Asia Region - Tehran site. There is also a web interface counter-part implemented for tps.

IOI Site Administration System, site-admin.
with Amin Khashkhashi Moghaddam
A web-interface to monitor and manage the nodes in a programming contest hall. site-admin integrates the data of participants and physical nodes, and it allows you to perform personalized bash or python scripts on the nodes remotely, and to monitor the progress.

PhyloTrees (github).
with Marina Knittel and Yusuf Alnawakhtha
An implementation of the Neighbor Joining algorithm for constructing Phylogenetic Trees. (A project in "Computational Genmoics" course)

HelliJudge, or jux.
with Mahrud Sayrafi
A programming competition judge with focus on security. The judge runs programs in a minimal chroot "jail" environment based on Fedora to simulate secure sand-boxing in pre-docker era.
